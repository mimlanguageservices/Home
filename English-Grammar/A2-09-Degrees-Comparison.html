Welcome to Degrees of Comparison!

Today we will learn how to use too, enough, very, and quite to describe things.

These words help us express the degree or intensity of an adjective.

too / enough / very / quite + adjective

Example: The coffee is very hot, but this one is too hot to drink!

Using "Too"

TOO + Adjective:

We use "too" to show that something is more than what is needed or wanted. It often has a negative meaning.

Subject + be + too + adjective

The soup is too salty. (I can't eat it.)

This box is too heavy. (I can't lift it.)

It's too cold to go outside.

Remember:

"Too" comes before the adjective.

Using "Enough"

Adjective + ENOUGH & ENOUGH + Noun:

"Enough" means sufficient, or the right amount.

adjective + enough

He is tall enough to reach the shelf.

The water is warm enough to swim.

enough + noun

I have enough money for the ticket.

There is enough food for everyone.

Using "Very"

VERY + Adjective:

"Very" makes an adjective stronger. It is a simple intensifier.

Subject + be + very + adjective

The movie was very interesting.

She is a very smart student.

It is very hot today.

Remember:

"Very" comes before the adjective.

Using "Quite"

QUITE + Adjective:

"Quite" means 'a little' or 'moderately'. It is less strong than "very".

Subject + be + quite + adjective

The test was quite difficult, but not impossible.

The weather is quite nice today.

I'm quite tired after the long walk.

Remember:

"Quite" also comes before the adjective.

Comparison

Let's compare them:

Word Meaning Example
very High degree The water is very cold.
quite Medium degree The water is quite cold.
too More than necessary (negative) The water is too cold to swim.
enough Sufficient amount The water is not warm enough.

Common Mistakes

Mistake 1: "Too" vs "Very"

❌ I am too happy. → ✅ I am very happy.

("Too" usually implies a problem.)

Mistake 2: Position of "Enough"

❌ He is enough tall. → ✅ He is tall enough.

("Enough" comes after an adjective.)

Mistake 3: "Enough" with nouns

❌ I have money enough. → ✅ I have enough money.

("Enough" comes before a noun.)

Examples: "Too" and "Enough"

Using "Too":

This coffee is too hot to drink.
The music is too loud.
I am too tired to go out.
Using "Enough":

Are you warm enough?
We don't have enough time.
She is old enough to drive.

Examples: "Very" and "Quite"

Using "Very":

The book is very interesting.
He runs very fast.
They are very happy.
Using "Quite":

The film was quite good.
It's a quite difficult question.
I'm quite sure about it.

Reading: A Day in the City

Yesterday, my friend and I went to the city. The weather was quite nice, so we decided to walk. The city was very busy. There were so many people!

We wanted to visit a museum, but the line was too long. We didn't have enough time to wait. So, we went to a café instead. The coffee was very good, but my cake was too sweet.

Later, we went shopping. I saw a beautiful jacket, but it was too expensive. I didn't have enough money to buy it. My friend bought a book that was quite interesting. She was old enough to watch a movie rated for 18+, but I was not.
