The Verb "To Be"

Three forms in present tense

Subject Verb Example
I am I am happy.
He / She / It is She is tall.
You / We / They are They are students.

Rule

Use am with "I", is with singular subjects, and are with plural subjects.

Affirmative Form

Subject + am/is/are

I am a teacher.

You are my friend.

He is from Japan.

She is beautiful.

It is sunny.

We are students.

They are at home.

Contractions (Short Forms):

I am → I'm | You are → You're | He is → He's | She is → She's | It is → It's | We are → We're | They are → They're

Negative Form

Subject + am/is/are + not

I am not a doctor.

You are not late.

He is not from China.

She is not angry.

It is not expensive.

We are not tired.

They are not at school.

Negative Contractions:

am not → I'm not | is not → isn't | are not → aren't

Example: He isn't here. / They aren't students.

Question Form

Am/Is/Are + subject?

Am I late?

Are you ready?

Is he your brother?

Is she a teacher?

Is it cold?

Are we friends?

Are they from Spain?

Short Answers

Are you happy? → Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.

Is he a student? → Yes, he is. / No, he isn't.

Are they ready? → Yes, they are. / No, they aren't.

Common Mistakes

Mistake 1: Wrong verb form

I is happy ✗ → I am happy ✓

They is students ✗ → They are students ✓

Mistake 2: Wrong question word order

You are ready? ✗ → Are you ready? ✓

Mistake 3: Confusing it's and its

It's = It is (It's cold today.)

Its = possessive (The dog wags its tail.)

Affirmative Examples

I am a student. / I'm happy.

You are very kind. / You're my best friend.

He is tall. / He's from Canada.

She is a teacher. / She's very smart.

It is sunny. / It's a beautiful day.

We are students. / We're from Spain.

They are friends. / They're very nice.

Negative Examples

I am not tired. / I'm not angry.

You are not late. / You're not / You aren't wrong.

He is not at home. / He's not / He isn't here.

She is not a doctor. / She's not / She isn't busy.

It is not cold. / It's not / It isn't expensive.

We are not ready. / We're not / We aren't tired.

They are not students. / They're not / They aren't from Japan.

Question Examples

Am I late? → Yes, you are. / No, you aren't.

Are you happy? → Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.

Is he your teacher? → Yes, he is. / No, he isn't.

Is she from China? → Yes, she is. / No, she isn't.

Is it expensive? → Yes, it is. / No, it isn't.

Are we late? → Yes, we are. / No, we aren't.

Are they students? → Yes, they are. / No, they aren't.

Reading Practice

Read this story about Maria:

My New Friend

I am Sarah. I'm a student at Lincoln High School. I'm 16 years old.

This is my new friend, Maria. She's from Spain, but she isn't from Madrid. She's from Barcelona. She's very friendly and funny.

Are we in the same class? Yes, we are! We are both in Class 10A. Our teacher is Mr. Johnson. He is very kind, but he isn't easy. His lessons are difficult, but they are interesting.

Is Maria happy at our school? Yes, she is! We are happy to have her here.
