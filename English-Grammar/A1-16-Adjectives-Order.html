Welcome to Adjectives!

Today we will learn about ADJECTIVES in English!

Adjectives describe nouns and tell us about their quality, size, age, color, and more.

Adjective(s) + Noun

Example: a beautiful big old red Italian silk scarf

What are adjectives?

Adjectives are words that describe or modify nouns. They give more information about size, shape, age, color, origin, or material.

Adjective Order (OSASCOMP)

When using multiple adjectives, follow this order:

Opinion - Size - Age - Shape - Color - Origin - Material - Purpose
Category Examples Example Sentence
Opinion beautiful, ugly, nice, good a beautiful dress
Size big, small, large, tiny a big house
Age old, young, new, ancient an old car
Shape round, square, long, thin a round table
Color red, blue, green, black a red apple
Origin Italian, Chinese, French an Italian restaurant
Material wooden, plastic, silk, metal a wooden chair

Memory Tip:

Remember: OSASCOMP - Opinion, Size, Age, Shape, Color, Origin, Material, Purpose

Adjective Order Examples

Applying the OSASCOMP rule:

Example 1:

a beautiful big old red Italian silk scarf

Opinion → Size → Age → Color → Origin → Material

Example 2:

a lovely small blue plastic toy

Opinion → Size → Color → Material

Example 3:

an expensive new black German car

Opinion → Age → Color → Origin

Note:

You don't always need all categories! Use only the adjectives that are necessary to describe the noun.

Adjective Agreement

Adjectives in English DO NOT change for plural nouns

Adjectives stay the SAME for singular and plural nouns
Singular Plural Rule
a red car two red cars ✓ Adjective stays the same
a big house three big houses ✓ No -s on adjective
a beautiful flower many beautiful flowers ✓ Adjective never changes

Important Rule:

✓ a red apple → red apples (CORRECT)
✗ a red apple → reds apples (WRONG - Never add -s to adjectives!)

Position of Adjectives

Adjectives usually come BEFORE the noun

Adjective + Noun

Correct Position:

✓ a beautiful house
✓ an expensive car
✓ a happy child

Adjectives after "to be":

Adjectives can also come after the verb "to be" (am/is/are)

The house is beautiful.
The car is expensive.
The child is happy.

Remember:

• Before noun: a beautiful house
• After "to be": The house is beautiful
• Both are correct!

Common Mistakes with Adjectives

❌ Mistake 1: Adding -s to adjectives (plural agreement)

❌ three reds cars → ✅ three red cars

❌ many bigs houses → ✅ many big houses

❌ Mistake 2: Wrong adjective order

❌ a red big car → ✅ a big red car (size before color)

❌ an Italian beautiful dress → ✅ a beautiful Italian dress (opinion before origin)

❌ Mistake 3: Adjective after noun (English is NOT like Spanish/French)

❌ a car red → ✅ a red car

❌ a house big → ✅ a big house

❌ Mistake 4: Using "very" between adjectives

❌ a big very red car → ✅ a very big red car

Note: "very" modifies ONE adjective, not multiple!

Adjective Combinations

Multiple adjectives describing nouns:

Describing Cars:

a beautiful big old red Italian sports car
an expensive small new black German car

Describing Houses:

a lovely large old brick house
a nice small new white wooden house

Describing Clothes:

a beautiful long blue silk dress
an ugly big brown wool sweater

Describing Objects

Using adjectives to describe everyday objects:

In the Kitchen:

a large round wooden table
a small sharp metal knife
a beautiful old Chinese vase
some clean white ceramic plates
In the Bedroom:

a comfortable big soft bed
a small square black clock
a beautiful antique wooden dresser
some thick warm cotton blankets
In the Living Room:

a comfortable old leather sofa
a modern flat-screen TV
a beautiful green Persian rug
some small decorative glass vases
In the Office:

a heavy old metal filing cabinet
a new black plastic keyboard
a large rectangular glass desk
some expensive German pens
Notice:

All adjectives follow the OSASCOMP order and stay the same (no -s) even with plural nouns!

Describing People

Using adjectives to describe people:

Physical Appearance:

She is a beautiful tall young blonde woman.
He is a handsome short middle-aged man.
They are cute little children.

Personality:

My friend is a kind and friendly person.
My teacher is a patient and helpful woman.
My brother is a smart young student.

Mixed Descriptions:

She is a funny tall young Japanese girl.
He is a serious old British man.

Reading: My Dream Bedroom

A Description of My Perfect Room

I have always dreamed of having the perfect bedroom. In my imagination, it is a beautiful large bright room with big windows.

The walls are painted in a soft light blue color that makes me feel calm and relaxed. There is a comfortable big soft bed in the center with clean white cotton sheets.

On one wall, there is a beautiful old wooden bookshelf filled with my favorite books. Next to it is a small round modern reading lamp.

The floor has a thick warm Persian rug in beautiful red and gold colors. In the corner, there is a comfortable old leather reading chair.

On my desk, I have a new black metal laptop and some expensive German pens. The desk is made of strong dark oak wood.

The windows have long thin white silk curtains that move gently in the breeze. This is my dream bedroom!

Analysis of Adjectives

Adjectives in correct order (OSASCOMP):

• beautiful large bright room (opinion + size + opinion)

• soft light blue color (shape + age + color)

• comfortable big soft bed (opinion + size + opinion)

• clean white cotton sheets (opinion + color + material)

• beautiful old wooden bookshelf (opinion + age + material)

• small round modern lamp (size + shape + age)

More adjective combinations:

• thick warm Persian rug (size + opinion + origin)

• beautiful red and gold colors (opinion + color + color)

• comfortable old leather chair (opinion + age + material)

• new black metal laptop (age + color + material)

• long thin white silk curtains (size + shape + color + material)

Notice:

All adjectives come BEFORE the noun. Multiple adjectives follow OSASCOMP order. All adjectives stay the same form (no -s added)!
