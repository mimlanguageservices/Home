Welcome to Past Simple vs. Present Perfect!

Understanding when to use PAST SIMPLE vs PRESENT PERFECT

The key question: Is there a connection to NOW?

Past Simple Present Perfect
VERB + ED / Irregular HAVE/HAS + PAST PARTICIPLE
Finished time in the past Connected to now
I went to Paris last year I have been to Paris

Rule 1: Finished Time = Past Simple

Use Past Simple when:

✓ We mention a specific finished time

✓ The action is complete and disconnected from now

✓ Time words: yesterday, last week, in 2010, ago

Timeline

Last week
✗ Finished
NOW

Examples:

• I visited Paris last year.

• She graduated in 2020.

• They met three days ago.

Rule 2: Connection to Now = Present Perfect

Use Present Perfect when:

✓ We DON'T mention when (time is not important)

✓ The result or experience is relevant NOW

✓ The time period is still continuing

✓ Time words: ever, never, already, yet, recently, just

Timeline

Sometime
❓ Unknown
✓ NOW

Examples:

• I have visited Paris. (experience)

• She has graduated from university. (result now)

• They have met before. (relevant now)

Rule 3: Finished vs. Continuing Time Periods

Finished Time Periods
(Past Simple)		 Continuing Time Periods
(Present Perfect)
yesterday today
last week/month/year this week/month/year
in 2010 ever/never
three days ago already/yet/just
when I was young recently/lately

Compare:

✗ I saw three movies this week. (week not finished yet!)

✓ I have seen three movies this week.

✓ I saw three movies last week. (last week is finished)

Rule 4: Questions with "When" and "Ever"

Important Rule:

If you ask "When?" → Use Past Simple

If you ask "Have you ever?" → Use Present Perfect

Conversation Example:

A: "Have you ever been to Japan?"

→ Present Perfect (general experience)

B: "Yes, I have."

→ Present Perfect

A: "When did you go?"

→ Past Simple (asking for specific time)

B: "I went there in 2019."

→ Past Simple (giving specific time)

Remember:

Once you mention a specific time, switch to Past Simple!

Common Mistakes to Avoid

✗ Mistake 1: Past Simple with continuing time

✗ I saw him today.

✓ I have seen him today. (today is not finished yet)

✗ Mistake 2: Present Perfect with specific past time

✗ I have visited London last year.

✓ I visited London last year. (last year is finished)

✗ Mistake 3: Present Perfect with "ago"

✗ I have finished my homework two hours ago.

✓ I finished my homework two hours ago. ("ago" = Past Simple)

✗ Mistake 4: "When" with Present Perfect

✗ When have you arrived?

✓ When did you arrive? ("When" = Past Simple)

Examples: Contrasts in Context

Present Perfect:

Experience, time not specified

Yesterday

Past Simple:

Yesterday is finished

This Week

This week is still continuing

Past Simple:

Specific finished event

Ever in Life

Extended Conversation Example

A Conversation About Travel

John: "Have you ever been to Italy?"

→ Present Perfect (asking about life experience)

Sarah: "Yes, I have. I went there last summer."

→ Present Perfect to answer, then Past Simple with specific time

John: "Really? Where did you go?"

→ Past Simple (asking about the specific trip)

Sarah: "I visited Rome and Florence. It was amazing!"

→ Past Simple (describing finished past events)

John: "Lucky you! I have never been to Italy, but I have always wanted to go."

→ Present Perfect (life experiences up to now)

Notice:

The conversation starts with Present Perfect (general experience), then switches to Past Simple when specific times are mentioned!

News vs. History

Recent News (Present Perfect)

News often uses Present Perfect for recent events that are still relevant:

• Scientists have discovered a new planet.

Recent news, connected to now

• The president has announced new policies.

Recent announcement, still relevant

• Three people have been injured in an accident.

Recent event with current relevance

History (Past Simple)

History uses Past Simple for finished past events:

• Columbus discovered America in 1492.

Historical fact with specific date

• The president announced new policies last month.

Past announcement with specific time

• Three people were injured in yesterday's accident.

Past event with specific time reference

Reading: A Day I'll Never Forget

Past Simple Story

Last summer, I went on the most amazing trip of my life. On July 15th, I flew to New York City for the first time. When I arrived at the airport, I felt so excited.

The next day, I visited the Statue of Liberty. The weather was perfect. I took hundreds of photos and bought some souvenirs. In the evening, I ate dinner in Times Square. The food was delicious!

On my last day, I walked across the Brooklyn Bridge. It was such a wonderful experience. I returned home on July 20th with so many great memories.

→ Why Past Simple? All events are finished and have specific times.

Reading: My Life Experiences

Present Perfect Story

I have traveled to many amazing places in my life. I have visited three different continents, and I have met people from all over the world.

I have always loved adventure. I have tried many exciting activities like skydiving and scuba diving. I have never been afraid to try new things.

Recently, I have become more interested in cultural experiences. This year, I have learned to cook dishes from different countries. I have also started learning a new language.

My experiences have made me who I am today. They have taught me so much about the world and about myself. I have grown as a person, and I can't wait to have more adventures!

→ Why Present Perfect? Experiences without specific times, still relevant to who the person is now.

Writing Practice

Write two paragraphs:

Paragraph 1: A Specific Past Experience

Write about a trip or event at a specific time. Use Past Simple. Include:

  • When it happened
  • Where you went
  • What you did
  • How you felt

Paragraph 2: Your Life Experiences

Write about experiences you've had (no specific times). Use Present Perfect. Include:

  • Places you have visited
  • Things you have done
  • Skills you have learned
  • How experiences have affected you
