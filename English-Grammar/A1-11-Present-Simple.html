Welcome to Present Simple!

Today we will learn about the Present Simple tense!

We use it to talk about habits, facts, and routines.

We'll also learn frequency adverbs like:

always usually often sometimes rarely never

Present Simple: Forms

Structure:

Subject Verb Form Example
I / You / We / They base form I work. / They play.
He / She / It base form + s/es He works. / She plays.

Rule:

Add -s or -es to the verb for he/she/it (third person singular).

Spelling Rules for Third Person

How to add -s or -es:

1. Most verbs: Add -s

work → works | play → plays | eat → eats

2. Verbs ending in -s, -sh, -ch, -x, -o: Add -es

watch → watches | go → goes | miss → misses

3. Verbs ending in consonant + y: Change y → ies

study → studies | try → tries | fly → flies

4. Irregular: have → has

When to Use Present Simple?

1. HABITS & ROUTINES

I drink coffee every morning.

She goes to the gym three times a week.

They watch TV in the evening.

2. GENERAL FACTS & TRUTHS

The sun rises in the east.

Water boils at 100°C.

Cats like milk.

3. PERMANENT SITUATIONS

I live in Tokyo.

He works at a bank.

She speaks three languages.

Negative Form

Structure: Subject + do/does + not + base verb

Subject Negative Form Example
I / You / We / They don't (do not) I don't work on Sundays.
He / She / It doesn't (does not) She doesn't like coffee.
Important:

After don't/doesn't, use the base form of the verb (no -s/-es)!

✅ He doesn't work. (correct)
❌ He doesn't works. (wrong!)

Frequency Adverbs

What are Frequency Adverbs?

Words that tell us how often something happens.

Frequency Scale (100% → 0%)

100% always
→
usually
→
often
→
sometimes
→
rarely
→
0% never

Meanings:

Adverb Frequency Example
always 100% I always brush my teeth.
usually ~80% She usually drinks tea.
often ~60% We often go to the park.
sometimes ~40% He sometimes reads books.
rarely ~10% They rarely eat out.
never 0% I never smoke.

Position of Frequency Adverbs

Rule: BEFORE the main verb

Subject + frequency adverb + verb

I always wake up early.
She usually drinks coffee.
We sometimes go swimming.

Special Rule: AFTER the verb "to be"

Subject + be + frequency adverb

He is always late.
They are usually happy.
I am never bored.
Summary:

Before main verbs, but AFTER the verb "to be"!

Daily Routine Examples

Using Present Simple with Frequency Adverbs:

I always wake up at 7 AM.

She usually has breakfast at home.

He often takes the bus to work.

We sometimes go out for dinner.

They rarely watch TV.

I never eat fast food.

Jobs and Activities:

Positive:

I work from home.
She teaches English.
They play tennis.
Negative:

I don't work weekends.
She doesn't teach math.
They don't play football.

Common Mistakes

❌ Mistake 1: Forgetting -s/-es with he/she/it

❌ She work at a bank. → ✅ She works at a bank.

❌ He study every day. → ✅ He studies every day.

❌ Mistake 2: Adding -s after doesn't/don't

❌ He doesn't likes pizza. → ✅ He doesn't like pizza.

❌ She doesn't works. → ✅ She doesn't work.

❌ Mistake 3: Wrong position of frequency adverb

❌ I eat always breakfast. → ✅ I always eat breakfast.

❌ Always he is late. → ✅ He is always late.

Reading: My Daily Routine

My name is Tom and I usually have a busy routine. I always wake up at 6:30 AM. I brush my teeth and take a shower. Then I eat breakfast.

I usually drink coffee and read the news. I don't eat a big breakfast because I don't have much time.

I work at a school. I teach English to young students. My classes start at 9 AM. I often stay at school until 5 PM.

After work, I sometimes go to the gym. I like exercise because it helps me relax. I never skip my workout on Mondays.

In the evening, I usually cook dinner at home. My wife and I watch TV or talk about our day. We rarely go out on weekdays because we are tired. We always go to bed before 11 PM.

Story Analysis

Present Simple Verbs:

• I wake up, brush, take, eat, drink, read

• I work, I teach

• My classes start (third person: classes)

• It helps me (third person: help + s)

• I don't eat, I don't have (negatives)

Frequency Adverbs in the Story:

• always: wake up, go to bed (100%)

• usually: have busy routine, drink coffee, cook dinner (80%)

• often: stay at school (60%)

• sometimes: go to gym (40%)

• rarely: go out on weekdays (10%)

• never: skip workout on Mondays (0%)
