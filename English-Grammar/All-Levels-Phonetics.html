English Sound Patterns

Understanding How English Sounds Work

English has predictable patterns for how letters sound. Learning these patterns helps you read and pronounce words correctly!

Long Vowels Short Vowels Diphthongs R-Controlled Silent Letters

1. Short Vowels (Quick Sounds)

Short vowels make quick, simple sounds. They usually appear in words with one vowel in the middle.

a as in cat, hat, mat (sounds like "ah")

e as in bed, pet, red (sounds like "eh")

i as in sit, hit, pit (sounds like "ih")

o as in hot, pot, dot (sounds like "ah" or "oh")

u as in cup, run, sun (sounds like "uh")

2. Long Vowels (Say Their Name)

Long vowels "say their name" - they sound like the letter itself. Often with silent 'e' at the end!

a as in cake, make, name (sounds like "ay")

e as in even, me, she (sounds like "ee")

i as in bike, kite, like (sounds like "eye")

o as in home, hope, note (sounds like "oh")

u as in cute, use, mute (sounds like "yoo")

3. Diphthongs (Gliding Sounds)

Diphthongs are two vowel sounds that blend together smoothly in one syllable.

oi/oy as in boy, toy, coin

ou/ow as in cow, how, loud

au/aw as in saw, paw, autumn

ai/ay as in rain, day, play

oo as in moon, book, food

4. R-Controlled Vowels

When 'r' comes after a vowel, it changes the vowel's sound. The 'r' "controls" it!

ar as in car, star, far

er as in her, never, under

ir as in bird, first, girl

or as in for, storm, corn

ur as in turn, burn, nurse

5. Silent Letters

Some letters don't make any sound! We write them but don't say them.

Silent e: make, home, take

Silent k: knife, know, knee

Silent w: write, wrong, answer

Silent b: lamb, climb, thumb

Silent h: honest, hour, ghost

Complete Word Examples

Common Words with Color-Coded Sounds

apple - Short 'a' sound

grape - Long 'a' sound with silent 'e'

rain - 'ai' diphthong

car - R-controlled vowel

knife - Silent 'k' and 'e', long 'i'

toy - 'oy' diphthong

Word Families

Words That Follow the Same Pattern

-ake Family (long 'a'):

bake, cake, make, lake, take

-ight Family (long 'i'):

night, light, right, fight, sight

Tricky Words

Some words break the rules!

English has irregular words that don't follow normal patterns. You just have to memorize these:

said - sounds like "sed" (not "sayd")

busy - 'u' sounds like short 'i'

enough - 'ough' sounds like "uff"

through - 'ough' sounds like "oo"

people - 'eo' sounds like "ee"

Reading Practice

Read the text and notice the different sound patterns:

The Rainy Day

One day, Jake woke up late. He looked outside and saw rain falling on the street. He made himself a hot cup of tea and some toast. Jake knew he couldn't go to the park today. Instead, he decided to write a letter to his friend and read his new book about cars.

Sound Patterns in the Story

Let's identify the sound patterns:

Long Vowels: Jake, late, made, tea

Short Vowels: one, hot, cup

Diphthongs: rain, day, new, book

R-Controlled: looked, park, cars, letter

Silent Letters: knew, write, make
