Welcome to Time is Money!

Today we will learn how to use WASTE, SPEND, SAVE with TIME and MONEY!

In English, we talk about TIME and MONEY in very similar ways.

TIME = MONEY

Example: I wasted my time. / I wasted my money.

Key Verbs:

• Waste: Use badly (negative)

• Spend: Use generally (neutral)

• Save: Keep for later (positive)

WASTE - Time & Money

WASTE = Use badly, use for nothing important

SUBJECT + waste + time/money

Meaning:

• To use time or money badly

• To use time or money for something useless

• This verb has a NEGATIVE feeling

• You regret wasting time or money

Grammar Patterns:

Pattern Time Example Money Example
waste + time/money I wasted my time. I wasted my money.
waste + time/money + on I wasted my time on that game. I wasted my money on that car.
waste + time/money + -ing I wasted my time watching TV. I wasted my money buying junk.

Time: I wasted 3 hours playing video games. I should have studied!

Money: I wasted $100 on lottery tickets. I should have saved it!

Remember!

"Waste" is ALWAYS negative. You feel bad about wasting time or money.

SPEND - Time & Money

SPEND = Use (neutral, just a fact)

SUBJECT + spend + time/money

Meaning:

• To use time or money

• This verb is NEUTRAL (not good or bad)

• Just stating a fact about how you used time/money

• Can be good, bad, or just normal

Grammar Patterns:

Pattern Time Example Money Example
spend + time/money I spent 2 hours there. I spent $50.
spend + time/money + on I spent 2 hours on homework. I spent $50 on groceries.
spend + time/money + -ing I spent 2 hours studying. I spent $50 buying food.
spend + time + with I spent time with my family. ❌ (only for time!)

Time: I spend 8 hours a day at work. (neutral fact)

Money: I spend $500 a month on rent. (neutral fact)

Important!

"Spend time WITH someone" is a special pattern - you can't say "spend money with"!

SAVE - Time & Money

SAVE = Keep for later, not use now

SUBJECT + save + time/money

Meaning:

• To keep time or money for later

• To not waste time or money

• This verb has a POSITIVE feeling

• You are being smart and efficient

Grammar Patterns:

Pattern Time Example Money Example
save + time/money This app saves time. Coupons save money.
save + time/money + by -ing I saved time by taking a taxi. I saved money by cooking at home.
save + time/money + for Save time for important things. I'm saving money for a car.

Time: I saved 30 minutes by driving instead of walking.

Money: I saved $20 by using a coupon.

Remember!

"Save" is positive! It means you are wise with your time or money.

Other Important Verbs

More verbs we use with TIME and MONEY:

Verb Meaning Time Example Money Example
invest Use now to get benefits later I invested time in learning English. I invested money in stocks.
lose Can't get it back I lost time because of traffic. I lost money in the casino.
run out of Have no more We're running out of time! I'm running out of money!
make Create/earn Make time for your family. I make money from my job.
have Possess I don't have time. I don't have money.

Special Expressions:

Time Expressions:

• Kill time (waste time waiting)
• Take your time (don't hurry)
• Time flies (time passes quickly)
• No time to lose (must hurry)
Money Expressions:

• Throw money away (waste money)
• Money talks (money has power)
• Easy money (money earned easily)
• Money doesn't grow on trees

Common Mistakes

Mistake 1: Wrong preposition after "spend"

✗ I spent my time in video games.

✓ I spent my time on video games.

Note: Use "on" or "-ing", not "in"!

Mistake 2: Using "waste" with "for"

✗ I wasted money for that game.

✓ I wasted money on that game.

Note: Use "waste + on" or "waste + -ing"!

Mistake 3: Confusing "spend" and "waste"

✗ I spend my time every day. (sounds like you waste it)

✓ I spend 8 hours every day at work. (neutral, needs details)

Note: "Spend" needs context to know if it's good or bad!

Mistake 4: Wrong verb form

✗ I wasted money to buy that.

✓ I wasted money buying that.

Note: Use "-ing" after waste/spend/save, not "to"!

Mistake 5: Mixing time and money patterns

✗ I spent money with my friends. (sounds like time)

✓ I spent time with my friends. (time only!)

✓ I spent money on dinner with my friends. (money needs "on")

WASTE Examples - Time & Money

Remember: WASTE = bad use (negative feeling)

TIME - Waste:

• I wasted 2 hours waiting for the bus.
• Don't waste your time on that show.
• He wasted the whole day sleeping.
• Stop wasting time scrolling social media!
• We wasted 30 minutes looking for parking.
• I feel like I wasted my youth partying.
MONEY - Waste:

• I wasted $50 on that broken phone.
• Don't waste your money on lottery tickets.
• She wasted hundreds of dollars on clothes she never wears.
• Stop wasting money eating out every day!
• We wasted $100 on tickets to a bad concert.
• I feel like I wasted my savings on that car.

Common Collocations:

TIME: waste time waiting / waste time on social media / waste time worrying / waste precious time

MONEY: waste money on junk / waste hard-earned money / waste money buying useless things

Notice!

After "waste", we often explain WHY it was a waste or WHAT we wasted it on!

SPEND Examples - Time & Money

Remember: SPEND = use (neutral, just stating facts)

TIME - Spend:

• I spend 2 hours commuting every day.
• We spent the weekend at the beach.
• She spends a lot of time with her family.
• How do you spend your free time?
• I spent 3 years studying medicine.
• They spend every summer in Europe.
MONEY - Spend:

• I spend $500 on rent every month.
• We spent $2000 on our vacation.
• She spends a lot of money on books.
• How much do you spend on groceries?
• I spent $30,000 on my education.
• They spend thousands on Christmas gifts.

Special Pattern - SPEND TIME WITH:

• I want to spend more time with you.

• She spends quality time with her children.

• We spent the whole evening with our neighbors.

• I don't spend enough time with my parents.

Remember!

"Spend time WITH" is only for time! You cannot say "spend money with"!

SAVE Examples - Time & Money

Remember: SAVE = keep for later, be efficient (positive)

TIME - Save:

• This shortcut saves 10 minutes.
• I saved time by preparing lunch yesterday.
• Online shopping saves a lot of time.
• You can save time by planning ahead.
• This machine saves hours of work.
• I need to save time for my hobbies.
MONEY - Save:

• This coupon saves $10.
• I saved money by cooking at home.
• Online shopping saves a lot of money.
• You can save money by buying generic brands.
• I'm saving money for a new laptop.
• I need to save money for retirement.

Pattern: SAVE by -ing

TIME: I saved time by taking the express train.

MONEY: I saved money by walking instead of taking a taxi.

Pattern: SAVE for

TIME: Save some time for yourself! / I'm saving time for my family.

MONEY: I'm saving money for a vacation. / She's saving for a house.

Reading: A Day of Regrets

My Terrible Day

Yesterday was one of the worst days ever. I wasted so much time and money, and now I regret everything!

First, I wasted 3 hours in the morning scrolling through social media. I should have spent that time studying for my exam. Then, I decided to go shopping to relax. Big mistake! I wasted $200 on clothes I don't even need. I should have saved that money for my rent.

In the afternoon, I spent 2 hours at the coffee shop with my friend Maria. That was nice - I always enjoy spending time with her. But then I wasted another hour waiting for a bus that never came. I finally took a taxi and wasted $30 on the ride. I could have saved money by taking the subway!

When I got home, I realized I had spent the entire day doing useless things. I didn't study, I didn't exercise, and I didn't prepare for tomorrow's meeting. I wasted my whole day and wasted my money on things I didn't need.

Now I'm trying to save time by preparing everything tonight. I'm also going to start saving money every week so I don't run out again. Tomorrow, I promise to spend my time more wisely. I want to invest my time in important things like studying and exercising, not waste it on my phone!

My goal is to stop wasting time and start making the most of every minute. Time is money, and I can't afford to waste either one anymore!

Reading Analysis

WASTE Examples (Negative - Bad Use):

• wasted so much time and money - both used badly

• wasted 3 hours scrolling social media - unproductive

• wasted $200 on clothes - unnecessary spending

• wasted another hour waiting for bus - time lost

• wasted $30 on taxi - could have saved

• wasted my whole day - completely unproductive

• wasted my money - spent on useless things

• wasting time - continuing bad habit

SPEND Examples (Neutral - Just Facts):

• spent that time studying - how time should be used

• spent 2 hours at coffee shop - neutral fact

• spending time with her - special pattern with people

• spent the entire day - describing how day was used

• spend my time more wisely - future plan

SAVE Examples (Positive - Smart Use):

• saved that money for rent - keep for important things

• saved money by taking subway - make smart choices

• save time by preparing tonight - be efficient

• saving money every week - building for future

Other Verbs:

• invest my time - use time for long-term benefits

• making the most of every minute - use time well

• run out - have no more money
