What are Adverbs?

Adverbs are words that describe verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.

They answer questions like: How? How often? Where?

He drives carefully.

"Carefully" is an adverb. It describes how he drives.

Adverbs of Frequency

They answer the question "How often?"

Examples:

always, usually, often, sometimes, never

I always drink coffee in the morning. (100%)

She usually walks to work. (90%)

He sometimes eats lunch at his desk. (50%)

They never go to the cinema. (0%)

Position: These adverbs usually go before the main verb.

Adverbs of Manner

They answer the question "How?"

Rule:

Many adverbs of manner end in -ly (e.g., slow -> slowly).

He speaks slowly.

She sings beautifully.

They work hard. (Irregular!)

Position: These adverbs usually go after the main verb or object.

Adverbs of Place

They answer the question "Where?"

Examples:

here, there, outside, inside, everywhere

Please come here.

The children are playing outside.

I looked for my keys everywhere.

Position: These adverbs usually go after the main verb or object.

Examples: Adverbs of Frequency

I am never late for class.

He often forgets his keys.

Do you usually eat breakfast?

Examples: Adverbs of Manner

The turtle walks slowly.

She speaks English well.

He closed the door quietly.

Examples: Adverbs of Place

I will wait for you there.

Let's go somewhere warm.

Is there a doctor nearby?

Reading: A Day at the Park

Yesterday, I went to the park. The sun was shining brightly. Children were playing happily everywhere. I sat on a bench and watched them. A dog ran quickly past me. I always feel relaxed at the park. I stayed there for an hour. Then I walked home slowly.

Writing Practice

Describe a Scene

Describe a place you know well (like your room, a park, or a cafe). Use adverbs of manner and place.

Example: People talk quietly inside the library. The books are organized neatly on the shelves.
