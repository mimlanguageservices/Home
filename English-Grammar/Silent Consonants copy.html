Welcome to Silent Consonants!

Today we will learn about SILENT CONSONANTS

Silent consonants are letters that are written but not pronounced.

Written ≠ Spoken  →  knife = /naɪf/

Example: The K in knife is silent — we say /naɪf/, not /knaɪf/.

Why do silent consonants exist?

• English spelling comes from Old English, Latin, French, and Greek

• Pronunciation changed over centuries, but spelling stayed the same

• Silent letters are a key reason English spelling can be difficult

Silent B and Silent C

Silent B — two common patterns:

Pattern Examples Pronunciation
-MB (end of word) bomb, climb, thumb, lamb, limb, dumb /bɒm/, /klaɪm/, /θʌm/
-BT debt, doubt, subtle /det/, /daʊt/
-MB (mid-word) crumb, tomb, womb /krʌm/, /tuːm/

Silent C — common patterns:

• SC- combinations: scene, scent, muscle, scissors

• -CT combinations: indict → /ɪnˈdaɪt/

• -CLE: muscle, crescent

Remember!

The B is almost always silent when it comes after M at the end of a word: thumb, lamb, bomb.

Silent D, G, and GH

Silent D

• Before G: bridge, edge, ledge

• In some words: handsome, handkerchief, sandwich

Silent G

• Before N (start of word): gnat, gnaw, gnarl

• Before N (end of word): sign, align, design, foreign, campaign

Silent GH

-IGHT words:

• light, night, right
• fight, might, sight
• bright, fright, slight
-OUGH words:

• dough, though, through
• although, eight
• high, neighbor

Silent H, K, and L

Silent H

• At the start: hour, honor, heir, honest

• After C: chaos, character, choir, echo, anchor

• Other: ghost, rhinoceros, spaghetti, thyme

Silent K — pattern: KN- at start of word

• knee, kneel, knew, knife, knight

• knit, knob, knock, knot, know, knuckle

Silent L — patterns: -ALM and -ALK

• -ALM: calm, palm, salmon, balm, psalm

• -ALK: talk, walk, chalk, stalk

• -OUL / -OUL: could, would, should, half, calf

Tip!

The K in KN- words has been silent since Old English. It was pronounced centuries ago!

Silent N, P, S, T, and W

Silent N — pattern: -MN at end

• autumn, column, condemn, damn, hymn, solemn

Silent P — pattern: PS- or PN- at start

• psychology, psychic, pseudonym, psalm, pneumatic, pneumonia

• Also: receipt, coup, corps

Silent S

• island, aisle, isle, Arkansas, Illinois, débris, rendezvous

Silent T — patterns: -STEN, -STLE

• castle, fasten, hustle, listen, rustle, whistle, often, soften

Silent W — patterns: WR- at start, or WH- words

• write, wrap, wreck, wrinkle, wrist, wrong

• who, whom, whose, whole, answer, sword, two

Key Patterns Summary

Learn the pattern, not just the word:

Pattern Silent Letter Examples
-MB B is silent at end thumb, bomb, climb, lamb
KN- K is silent at start knife, knee, know, knot
WR- W is silent at start write, wrap, wrist, wreck
GN- G is silent at start gnat, gnaw, gnarl
-MN N is silent at end column, autumn, hymn, damn
-ALM / -ALK L is silent palm, calm, talk, walk, chalk
PS- / PN- P is silent at start psychology, pneumonia, psalm
-STLE / -STEN T is silent castle, whistle, listen, fasten
Study Tip!

Focus on patterns, not individual words. Once you know KN- = silent K, all KN- words become easy!

Silent Consonants in Sentences

Can you spot the silent consonants?

• The knight used a knife to cut the knot.

→ Silent K in: knight, knife, knot

• I like to walk and talk under the calm palm trees.

→ Silent L in: walk, talk, calm, palm

• She wrote the wrong answer on her wrist.

→ Silent W in: wrote, wrong, wrist

• The ghost appeared at the castle every autumn.

→ Silent H (ghost), Silent T (castle), Silent N (autumn)

• She studied psychology and learned to listen carefully.

→ Silent P (psychology), Silent T (listen)

• Who took the lamb? We have serious doubts.

→ Silent W (who), Silent B (lamb), Silent B (doubts)

Reading: A Walk in the Countryside

It was a quiet autumn afternoon. Maya decided to take a long walk through the countryside. She grabbed her knife to cut some herbs and a small bag to collect things along the way.

She followed a path past a ruined castle, where a large black crow let out a sharp knock against the stone wall. Maya laughed — she knew crows were clever birds.

She sat down under a palm-like tree and started to listen to the sounds around her. The wind was calm and the air smelled like fresh herbs. She spotted a lamb near the fence and a small gnarly tree with bent branches.

Maya reached into her bag and pulled out a book about local psychology and folklore. She found a chapter about a ghost said to haunt the old ruins. It was a solemn and eerie story.

As the sun began to set, she used her wrist watch to check the time. She had been walking for two hours. She wrote in her journal: "A beautiful afternoon — not one moment was dull." Then she whistled a soft tune and headed home.

• knock / knew — silent K (KN-)

• palm / calm — silent L (-ALM)

• listen — silent T (-STEN)

• lamb — silent B (-MB)

• gnarly — silent G (GN-)

• psychology — silent P (PS-)

• ghost — silent H

• solemn — silent N (-MN)

• wrist / wrote — silent W (WR-)

• hours — silent H

• whistled — silent T (-STLE)

Takeaway:

• Silent consonants appear in everyday vocabulary — not just rare words

• Learning patterns (KN-, WR-, -MB) helps you recognize them quickly

• Reading aloud helps train your ear for correct pronunciation
