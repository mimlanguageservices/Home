Irregular Plurals

Some nouns don't follow the regular rules! They change completely or stay the same.

What are Irregular Plurals?

Irregular plurals are nouns that DON'T add -s or -es. They change in different ways or don't change at all!

man → men (NOT mans ✗)

child → children (NOT childs ✗)

sheep → sheep (stays the same!)

Remember:

You must memorize irregular plurals! There are no rules for them.

Type 1: Vowel Change

The vowel inside the word changes

Singular Plural Change
man men a → e
woman women a → e
foot feet oo → ee
tooth teeth oo → ee
goose geese oo → ee
mouse mice ou → i

Type 2: Completely Different

The whole word changes!

Singular Plural
child children
person people
ox oxen
Important:

"Person" becomes "people" (NOT "persons" in everyday English)

"Child" becomes "children" (NOT "childs")

Type 3: No Change (Same Form)

These nouns stay exactly the same!

Singular Plural Example
sheep sheep one sheep, two sheep
deer deer one deer, three deer
fish fish one fish, many fish
series series one series, two series
species species one species, five species
Remember:

Don't add -s to these words! They never change.

Special Case: Always Plural

Some nouns are ALWAYS plural (they don't have a singular form)

Clothing

• pants / trousers
• jeans
• shorts
• glasses / sunglasses
• scissors

Tools & Items

• scissors
• tweezers
• binoculars
• headphones

✓ My pants are blue. (NOT "My pant is blue")

✓ I need scissors. (NOT "I need a scissor")

✓ Where are my glasses? (NOT "Where is my glass?")

Tip:

Use "a pair of" to count these: "a pair of scissors", "two pairs of pants"

Summary: Irregular Plurals

Type 1: Vowel Change

man → men | woman → women | foot → feet | tooth → teeth

Type 2: Completely Different

child → children | person → people

Type 3: No Change

sheep → sheep | deer → deer | fish → fish

Type 4: Always Plural

pants, scissors, glasses (use "a pair of...")

Examples: Vowel Change

There is one man in the room.

There are three men in the room.

That woman is a doctor.

Those women are doctors.

My foot hurts.

My feet hurt.

I lost a tooth.

I brush my teeth every day.

Examples: Completely Different

The child is playing.

The children are playing.

One person is waiting.

Many people are waiting.

There are three childs here. ✗

There are three children here. ✓

Five persons came to the party. ✗

Five people came to the party. ✓

Examples: No Change

I see one sheep.

I see ten sheep.

A deer is in the forest.

Five deer are in the forest.

I caught a fish!

I caught three fish!

Notice:

The verb changes (is/are), but the noun stays the same!

Examples: Always Plural

✓ My pants are too long.

✗ My pant is too long.

✓ I need scissors to cut the paper.

✗ I need a scissor to cut the paper.

✓ Where are my glasses?

✗ Where is my glass? (This means drinking glass!)

Counting them:

• I bought a pair of jeans.

• He has two pairs of glasses.

• Can I borrow a pair of scissors?

Reading Practice

Read this story. Notice the irregular plural nouns:

A Day at the Farm

Yesterday, we visited my uncle's farm. There were many animals there! We saw twenty sheep eating grass. Five geese were swimming in the pond. Three deer came close to the fence.

My uncle told us that ten children from the school visited last week. They saw the animals and learned about farm life. One child was scared of the big animals!

There were also many people working on the farm. Two men were fixing the barn, and three women were feeding the chickens. One person was collecting eggs.

I had so much fun! I got my pants dirty playing with the animals. My feet were tired, but I didn't want to leave!

Find the Irregular Plurals

From the story "A Day at the Farm":

Irregular Plurals - Vowel Change:

• geese (goose) • feet (foot) • men (man) • women (woman)

Irregular Plurals - Different Word:

• children (child) • people (person)

No Change:

• sheep (sheep) • deer (deer)

Always Plural:

• pants

Writing Practice

Write about people and animals

Write sentences using irregular plural nouns. Try to use: children, people, men, women, sheep, fish, or deer.

Example: Many people visit the zoo. The children feed the sheep. Two men are fishing.
