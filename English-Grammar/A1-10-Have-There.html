HAVE/HAS vs THERE IS/ARE

Two different ways to describe things

HAVE/HAS = Talk about possession (what someone owns or has)

THERE IS/ARE = Talk about existence (what exists in a place)

I have a car. (The car belongs to me - possession)

There is a car in the garage. (A car exists there - existence)

HAVE / HAS

Forms

Subject Verb Example
I / You / We / They have I have a car.
He / She / It has She has a bike.

Usage: Possession & Characteristics

• Possessions: I have a phone.

• Family: We have two children.

• Physical features: I have blue eyes.

• Experiences: They have a problem.

HAVE/HAS: Negative & Questions

Negative Form

I don't have a car.

He doesn't have time.

They don't have any money.

Question Form

Do you have a pen?

Does she have a car?

Do they have children?

Short Answers

Do you have a dog? → Yes, I do. / No, I don't.

Does he have a job? → Yes, he does. / No, he doesn't.

THERE IS / THERE ARE

Forms

Form Use Example
There is Singular (one thing) There is a book on the table.
There are Plural (many things) There are three cats in the garden.

Usage: Existence in a Location

• There is a park near my house.

• There are five people in the room.

• There is a problem with the computer.

THERE IS/ARE: Negative & Questions

Negative Form

There isn't a hospital nearby.

There aren't any cookies left.

Question Form

Is there a bank near here?

Are there any questions?

Short Answers

Is there a café nearby? → Yes, there is. / No, there isn't.

Are there any parks? → Yes, there are. / No, there aren't.

Key Difference

HAVE/HAS = Possession

Focus on the owner (a specific person/thing)

✓ I have a car. (The car belongs to me)

✓ The house has three bedrooms. (The house contains them)

THERE IS/ARE = Existence

Focus on the location or existence

✓ There is a car in the garage. (A car exists there)

✓ There are three bedrooms in the house. (They exist in the house)

HAVE/HAS Examples

I have a question.

She has blue eyes.

We have a meeting today.

The car has four doors.

They don't have pets.

Does he have siblings?

THERE IS/ARE Examples

There is milk in the fridge.

There are ten students here.

Is there a problem?

There aren't any messages.

There's a spider on the wall!

Are there any questions?

Comparisons

HAVE/HAS (Possession) THERE IS/ARE (Existence)
I have two dogs. There are two dogs in the yard.
She has a problem. There is a problem with the plan.
We have many books. There are many books on the shelf.
The hotel has a pool. There is a pool at the hotel.

Reading Practice

Read this story:

My Neighborhood

I have a nice apartment in the city center. My apartment has two bedrooms and a big kitchen.

There is a park near my building. In the park, there are many trees and flowers. There is also a small playground.

My building has ten floors. There are four apartments on each floor. I have friendly neighbors. They have two dogs.

There are many shops on my street. There is a supermarket, a bakery, and a pharmacy. The supermarket has everything I need.

Story Analysis

HAVE/HAS (Possession):

• I have a nice apartment (I own it)

• My apartment has two bedrooms (it contains them)

• My building has ten floors

• The supermarket has everything

THERE IS/ARE (Existence):

• There is a park near my building (exists nearby)

• There are many trees (exist in the park)

• There are four apartments (exist on each floor)

• There are many shops (exist on the street)
