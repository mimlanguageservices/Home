The Power of Suffixes

A suffix is a group of letters added to the end of a word to change its grammatical function.

Root Word + suffix = New Word Class

Learn one root and you can build an entire word family:

produce → production → productive → productively
VERB NOUN ADJECTIVE ADVERB

Why does this matter?

• Instead of memorising 4 words separately, you learn 1 root and apply 4 rules.

• Suffixes signal the word's role in a sentence — verb, noun, adjective, or adverb.

• Recognising suffixes also helps you understand words you have never seen before.

Transformation 1: Verb → Noun

Add these suffixes to a verb to make a noun that describes the action, process, or result:

Suffix Examples Note
-tion / -ation produce → production
exhaust → exhaustion
motivate → motivation		 Most common. Often drop final -e or -ate.
-ment enjoy → enjoyment
embarrass → embarrassment
harass → harassment		 Simply add to the verb with no spelling change.
-ance / -ence appear → appearance
clear → clearance
exist → existence		 Choice of -ance or -ence must be memorised.
Spelling tip!

Verbs ending in -ate usually drop the whole -ate ending before adding -tion: motivate → motivation  |  renovate → renovation

Transformation 2: Adjective → Noun

Add these suffixes to an adjective to make a noun that describes a quality or state:

Suffix Examples Spelling rule
-ness lonely → loneliness
tired → tiredness
happy → happiness		 -y changes to -i before -ness
-ity / -ty stupid → stupidity
opportune → opportunity
clear → clarity		 Common with Latin-origin adjectives. Sometimes spelling changes significantly.
-cy immediate → immediacy
accurate → accuracy
urgent → urgency		 Common after adjectives ending in -te or -nt.
Watch out!

Some words change significantly: clear → clarity (not clearness). These must be learned. When in doubt, -ness almost always works as a fallback.

Transformation 3: Making Adjectives

Several suffixes turn nouns or verbs into adjectives:

Suffix Meaning Examples
-ous / -ious "full of" or "having" scandal → scandalous
rebel → rebellious
-ful "having" or "characterised by" respect → respectful
resource → resourceful
-ive "having a tendency to" persuade → persuasive
elude → elusive
-able / -ible "can be done" read → readable
enjoy → enjoyable
see → visible
-ant / -ent "doing" or "being" dominate → dominant
depend → independent

Transformation 4: Adjective → Adverb

This is the simplest rule in English word formation:

Adjective + -ly = Adverb
Simple adjectives:
deep → deeply
slow → slowly
clear → clearly
Adjectives ending in -ic:
dramatic → dramatically
financial → financially
scientific → scientifically
Adjectives ending in -y:
happy → happily
easy → easily
heavy → heavily
Exceptions to know:
good → well (not goodly)
fast → fast (no change)
hard → hard (no change)
Pattern:

Adjectives ending in -ic always add -ally (not just -ly): dramatic → dramatically, not dramaticly.

Transformation 5: Making Verbs

These suffixes turn nouns or adjectives into verbs:

Suffix Meaning Examples
-ate "to make" or "to cause" motivate, hesitate, renovate
-en "to become" or "to make more" dark → darken
strong → strengthen
bright → brighten
-ise / -ize "to make" or "to apply a method" modern → modernise
legal → legalise
priority → prioritise
-ify "to make" or "to cause to be" glory → glorify
electric → electrify
example → exemplify
UK vs US spelling:

British English prefers -ise (modernise, legalise), while American English uses -ize (modernize, legalize). Both are correct — just be consistent.

Complete Word Families

One root word, four forms. Notice how the suffix tells you the word's role in the sentence.

VERB NOUN ADJECTIVE ADVERB
produce production productive productively
embarrass embarrassment embarrassing embarrassingly
dramatise drama dramatic dramatically
clarify clarity clear clearly
strengthen strength strong strongly
modernise modernisation modern modernly
Notice!

Not every family is perfectly regular. Some forms use a different root (clear → clarity, not clearness). Use a dictionary to check unfamiliar families.

Key Spelling Rules

-y → -i before a suffix

happy → happiness
happy → happily
heavy → heavier
beauty → beautiful
Drop -e before a vowel suffix

produce → production
persuade → persuasive
elude → elusive
renovate → renovation
-ic adjectives → add -ally

dramatic → dramatically
scientific → scientifically
automatic → automatically
(Exception: public → publicly)
Double consonant — short vowels

run → running
big → bigger
begin → beginner
(Does not apply with multi-syllable stress on first syllable)

Suffixes in Context

The same root — different forms in a sentence:

The factory will produce goods. → The production line is efficient. → Workers are productive. → They work productively.

Spilling coffee can embarrass you. → It caused great embarrassment. → It was an embarrassing moment. → She laughed embarrassingly loudly.

Schools need to modernise. → The modernisation took two years. → New classrooms look modern. → The building is modernly designed.

Please clarify your answer. → We need more clarity. → His explanation was clear. → She spoke clearly.

How to identify the word class:

• Look at the suffix first: -tion, -ment, -ness, -ity → almost always a noun

• -ous, -ful, -ive, -able, -ent → almost always an adjective

• -ly at the end → usually an adverb (but friendly, lovely are adjectives!)

Reading: The Modern Workplace

The modernisation of the workplace has created new opportunities for workers around the world. Many companies now prioritise flexibility, allowing employees to work from home with greater independence.

Productivity has become a key focus for most organisations. Managers look for workers who show creativity and determination. The ability to communicate with clarity is considered essential, and those who can simplify complex ideas are highly valued.

However, this new environment brings its own challenges. Many workers report that the separation between work and home life has become blurred. Technical difficulties during online meetings can cause significant embarrassment, and employees must show resilience.

Thankfully, surveys show that most employees are deeply satisfied with remote work. The freedom to create a comfortable workspace has led to noticeable improvements in wellbeing and enjoyment of work.
