Two Types of Special Pronouns

1. Possessive (Ownership)

Show who owns something

my your his her

Example: This is my book.

Also: mine yours his hers

Example: This book is mine.

2. Demonstrative (Pointing)

Point to things (near/far)

this/these (near)

that/those (far)

Examples:

This is my pen. (near, singular)

Those are your books. (far, plural)

Possessive Adjectives

Come BEFORE a noun (my car, your house)

Subject Possessive Adjective Example
I my My name is John.
you your Your car is nice.
he his His book is here.
she her Her dog is cute.
it its Its color is blue.
we our Our house is big.
they their Their teacher is kind.
Important:

Possessive adjectives MUST have a noun after them!

✓ This is my book. | ✗ This is my.

Possessive Pronouns

Stand alone (NO noun after them)

Subject Possessive Pronoun Example
I mine This book is mine.
you yours That car is yours.
he his The pen is his.
she hers The bag is hers.
it — (no possessive pronoun for "it")
we ours This house is ours.
they theirs Those books are theirs.
Remember:

Possessive pronouns DON'T need a noun after them!

✓ This is mine. | ✗ This is mine book.

Possessive Adjective vs. Pronoun

Possessive Adjective
(+ noun)		 Possessive Pronoun
(no noun)
This is my pen. This pen is mine.
Is this your book? Is this book yours?
His car is red. The red car is his.
I like her dress. That dress is hers.
Our house is big. The big house is ours.
Their dog is cute. That cute dog is theirs.

This is my. ✗ (needs a noun!)

This is my book. ✓ or This is mine. ✓

Demonstrative Pronouns

Point to things based on distance and number

Distance Singular (one) Plural (many)
NEAR (close to you) this these
FAR (away from you) that those

👤 (You are here)

📗 This book (near, singular)

📗📗📗 These books (near, plural)

- - - - - - - - - - - - - -

📕 That book (far, singular)

📕📕📕 Those books (far, plural)

• This is my phone. (in my hand)

• These are my keys. (in my hand)

• That is your phone. (on the table)

• Those are your keys. (on the table)

Common Mistakes

Mistake 1: Using possessive adjective without a noun

This is my. ✗

This is my book. ✓ or This is mine. ✓

Mistake 2: Using possessive pronoun with a noun

This is mine book. ✗

This is my book. ✓ or This is mine. ✓

Mistake 3: Confusing its/it's

its = possessive (The dog loves its toy.)

it's = it is (It's raining today.)

Mistake 4: Using wrong demonstrative for distance

This is nice. ✗ (pointing to something far away)

That is nice. ✓ (pointing to something far away)

Examples: Possessive Adjectives

My name is Sarah. My sister is 10 years old.

What is your name? Is this your pen?

Tom loves his dog. His dog is very friendly.

Lisa is wearing her new dress. Her dress is beautiful.

The cat is eating its food. Its tail is long.

Our teacher is nice. Our classroom is big.

They love their parents. Their house is nearby.

Examples: Possessive Pronouns

This book is mine. (= my book)

That car is yours. (= your car)

The blue pen is his. (= his pen)

The red bag is hers. (= her bag)

This house is ours. (= our house)

Those bicycles are theirs. (= their bicycles)

Whose phone is this? It's mine / yours / his / hers.

Examples: Demonstratives (Near)

This is my book. (holding it)

This pen is blue. (pointing to a pen nearby)

I like this song! (the song playing now)

These are my friends. (people near me)

These apples are fresh. (apples nearby)

These shoes are comfortable. (wearing them)

Examples: Demonstratives (Far)

That is my house. (pointing to a house across the street)

That car is expensive. (car far away)

Do you see that bird? (in the sky)

Those are my parents. (people far away)

Those mountains are beautiful. (far in the distance)

I want those shoes. (in the store window)

Examples: Mixed Practice

This is my dog. That is yours.

These books are mine. Those books are his.

Is this your phone? No, mine is over there.

Her car is new. That old car is ours.

Their house is this one. Those apartments are theirs too.

Reading Practice

Read this story. Notice the possessive and demonstrative pronouns:

At School

This is my classroom. These are my classmates. That tall boy over there is Tom. He is my best friend. His desk is next to mine.

This is my teacher, Ms. Johnson. Her desk is at the front. Those books on her desk are ours. We use them every day.

Look at this notebook! It's mine. That blue notebook is yours, right? Oh, and those pens on the table - are they yours or his?

Our classroom has big windows. We can see the playground from here. That playground is where we play. Their classroom is smaller, but ours is perfect!

I love this school and all my friends here!

Find the Pronouns

From the story "At School":

Possessive Adjectives (before nouns):

• my (classroom, classmates, best friend, teacher, friends)

• his (desk)

• her (desk - twice)

• our (classroom)

• their (classroom)

Possessive Pronouns (stand alone):

• mine (It's mine, notebook is mine)

• ours (books are ours, ours is perfect)

• yours (notebook is yours, are they yours)

• his (or his)

Demonstrative Pronouns:

• this (near: my classroom, my teacher, notebook, school)

• these (near: my classmates)

• that (far: tall boy, playground, blue notebook)

• those (far: books, pens)
