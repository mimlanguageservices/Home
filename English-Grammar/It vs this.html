Welcome: "It" vs "This"!

Both THIS and IT can refer to things — but they are used in very different situations!

THIS = new information  |  pointing nearby  |  introducing

IT = already known  |  weather / time / distance  |  referring back
Use "THIS" to:

• introduce something new
• point to something nearby
• present someone or something
• highlight something in the moment
Use "IT" to:

• refer back to something already mentioned
• talk about weather, time, or distance
• describe a situation in general
• avoid repeating a noun
Key Idea!

"This" points forward — to something new or nearby. "It" points backward — to something we already know or have mentioned.

"This" — Introducing & Pointing

Use THIS when you are introducing something for the first time or pointing to it

Rule 1 — Introducing new information:

• This is my new car. (= I am showing it to you for the first time.)

• This is my friend, Carlos. (= I am introducing him.)

• This is a great opportunity. (= I am presenting the idea.)

Rule 2 — Pointing to something nearby:

• Can you pass me this pen? (= the pen close to me)

• I love this neighbourhood! (= the one we are in right now)

• Look at this photo! (= the photo I am holding or showing you)

Rule 3 — Telephone introductions:

• Hello, this is Maria speaking. (= formal phone introduction)

• Hi! This is your doctor's office calling. (= introducing who is calling)

Remember!

"This" often signals the very first time something enters the conversation. After that first mention, we switch to "it."

"It" — Referring Back

Use IT when the thing is already known or has already been mentioned

Once something has been introduced with "this" (or a noun), we replace it with "it" to avoid repeating ourselves.

• I bought a new phone. It is very fast. (= the phone we just mentioned)

• She showed me a painting. It was beautiful. (= the painting)

• We visited a museum. It took three hours. (= the visit)

The "this → it" switch:

• This is my dog. It is very friendly. ✓

• This is a great film. It won many awards. ✓

• I saw a new café. It looks amazing. ✓

Common Error!

I bought a new car. This is very fast. ✗ — once the car is mentioned, use "it," not "this." It is very fast. ✓

Impersonal "It"

IT is also used as an impersonal subject — "this" cannot replace it!

Weather:

• It's raining.
• It's very hot today.
• It was cloudy all week.
• It's going to snow.
Time & Date:

• It's 3 o'clock.
• It's Monday.
• It's almost midnight.
• It's the 14th of May.
Distance:

• It's far from here.
• It's about 10 km away.
• It's a long walk.
• It's only five minutes.
General Situations:

• It's important to practise.
• It was nice to meet you.
• It's difficult to explain.
• It seems like a good idea.
Key Rule!

In ALL these cases, "this" would be WRONG: "This is raining." ✗ / "This is 3 o'clock." ✗ — only "it" works here.

Side-by-Side Comparison

Feature "This" "It"
Introduces new information ✓ Yes ✗ No
Refers back to known information ✗ No ✓ Yes
Points to something nearby ✓ Yes ✗ No
Weather / time / distance ✗ No ✓ Yes
Telephone introductions ✓ Yes (formal) ✓ Yes (casual)
Presenting a person ✓ "This is John." ✗ No
General situations ("...is nice") ✓ "This is nice." ✓ "It is nice." (after mention)
Quick Memory Trick!

"This" = new & near. "It" = already known or impersonal. Ask yourself: "Am I introducing something, or referring back to it?"

Common Errors & How to Fix Them

Error 1 — Using "this" after already mentioning the noun:

✗ I saw a great film. This was two hours long.

✓ I saw a great film. It was two hours long.

Error 2 — Using "this" for weather or time:

✗ This is raining outside. I hate this!

✓ It is raining outside. I hate it!

Error 3 — Using "it" to introduce a person:

✗ It is my friend, Ana.

✓ This is my friend, Ana.

Error 4 — Using "it" to point to something nearby:

✗ Can I have it book on the table there?

✓ Can I have this book on the table here?

Always ask:

Is it new in the conversation, or have I mentioned it before? Is it weather / time / distance? These questions will almost always give you the right answer.

"This" — Example Sentences

Introducing & Pointing — "THIS"

• This is my new apartment. I moved in last week.

→ Introducing something for the first time.

• Can you sign this document, please?

→ Pointing to something nearby (the document is right here).

• This is my colleague, David. He works in marketing.

→ Presenting a person for the first time.

• Hello, this is Sarah from the front desk.

→ Telephone introduction — identifying yourself.

• I love this song! It has such a beautiful melody.

→ Pointing to the song playing right now. Then "it" refers back to it.

• This is exactly what I needed. Thank you so much!

→ Highlighting something just given or shown to the speaker.

"It" — Example Sentences

Referring Back & Impersonal Uses — "IT"

• I watched a documentary last night. It was about ocean life.

→ Refers back to the documentary already mentioned.

• It's snowing! Schools might close tomorrow.

→ Impersonal "it" for weather.

• It's almost midnight. We should go home.

→ Impersonal "it" for time.

• She gave me a book for my birthday. It took me a week to finish it.

→ Both "it"s refer back to the book.

• It's about 20 minutes from here by bus.

→ Impersonal "it" for distance.

• It's difficult to learn a new language as an adult.

→ Impersonal "it" for a general situation or fact.

"It" vs "This" — Spot the Difference

Compare these pairs — can you see why each choice is correct?

• This is the report I mentioned. — It needs to be submitted by Friday.

→ "This" introduces the report; "it" refers back to it in the next sentence.

• A: Have you tried the new Thai restaurant? — B: Yes! It was incredible!

→ Speaker A introduced it; Speaker B refers back with "it."

• This weather is terrible! I can't believe it's still raining.

→ "This weather" points to the current weather; "it's raining" is impersonal.

• I'd like you to meet someone. This is Professor Lin. It was her idea to start the project.

→ "This is" introduces; "it was" refers back to the idea.

• This is my favourite café. It's open until midnight.

→ "This" introduces the café; "it" refers back in the next clause.

Reading: A New City

Anna moved to a new city last month. On her first morning, she looked out of her window and said, "This is amazing! I can see a park from here." She got dressed and went outside to explore.

In the park, she noticed a small fountain. It had colourful tiles and water that sparkled in the sunlight. A man sitting on a nearby bench pointed to a café across the street. "This place has the best coffee in the city," he said. Anna decided to try it.

Inside, the waiter brought her a cup of coffee and a pastry. It was the best coffee she had ever tasted. She also noticed the wooden furniture. "This chair is so beautiful!" she thought. After paying, she called her sister. "Hello? This is Anna. You have to come and visit me here!"

Her sister replied, "How is the weather? Is it cold?" Anna laughed. "It's a perfect sunny day. It's about 22 degrees. It's only a short flight from you — please come!"

Color Code:

Blue = "this"  |  Green = "it" — Go to the next slide to analyse each example!

Reading Analysis

"This" and "It" found in the text:

THIS (blue) — introducing / pointing:

• "This is amazing!" — introducing her reaction to something new she can see

• "This place has the best coffee" — pointing to the nearby café

• "This chair is so beautiful!" — pointing to the chair right next to her

• "This is Anna." — telephone introduction

IT (green) — referring back / impersonal:

• "It had colourful tiles" — refers back to the fountain

• "decided to try it" — refers back to the café

• "It was the best coffee" — refers back to the coffee

• "Is it cold?" / "It's a perfect sunny day" / "It's 22 degrees" / "It's a short flight" — impersonal: weather, temperature, distance

Notice the pattern:

• Every time something enters the story for the first time → "this"

• Every time we refer back to something already mentioned → "it"

• Weather, temperature, and distance always use "it" — never "this"
