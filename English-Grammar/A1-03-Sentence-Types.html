Three Types of Sentences

In English, we use three main types of sentences:

Statements

Tell information

I like pizza.

Questions

Ask for information

Do you like pizza?

Negatives

Say "no" or "not"

I don't like pizza.

Statements

What is a Statement?

A statement tells information. It ends with a period (.).

I am a student.

She plays tennis.

They live in London.

Remember:

Statements use normal word order: Subject + Verb + Object

Questions

What is a Question?

A question asks for information. It ends with a question mark (?).

Are you a student?

Does she play tennis?

Do they live in London?

Remember:

Questions often start with: Do, Does, Is, Are, Can, etc.

Negatives

What is a Negative?

A negative sentence says "no" or "not". We use "not" or contractions (don't, doesn't, isn't, aren't).

I am not a teacher. or I'm not a teacher.

She does not play tennis. or She doesn't play tennis.

They do not live in London. or They don't live in London.

Remember:

Use "not" after the helping verb (am, is, are, do, does, can, etc.)

Examples: Statements

I eat breakfast every day.

My brother works in a hospital.

We study English at school.

The cat sleeps on the sofa.

Examples: Questions

Do you eat breakfast every day?

Does your brother work in a hospital?

Do we study English at school?

Does the cat sleep on the sofa?

Examples: Negatives

I don't eat breakfast every day.

My brother doesn't work in a hospital.

We don't study English at school.

The cat doesn't sleep on the sofa.

Comparing All Three Types

She likes pizza. (Statement)

Does she like pizza? (Question)

She doesn't like pizza. (Negative)

They play football. (Statement)

Do they play football? (Question)

They don't play football. (Negative)

Reading Practice

Read this conversation. Notice the different sentence types:

At the Café

Tom: Hi, Sarah! Do you like coffee?
Sarah: Yes, I do. I drink coffee every morning.
Tom: Do you like tea?
Sarah: No, I don't. I don't like tea.
Tom: I love tea! Do you want some cake?
Sarah: Yes, please. I love chocolate cake!

Identify the Sentence Types

From the conversation:

Statements:

• I drink coffee every morning.

• I love tea!

• I love chocolate cake!

Questions:

• Do you like coffee?

• Do you like tea?

• Do you want some cake?

Negatives:

• I don't like tea.
