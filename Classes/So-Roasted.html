What is a Comedy Roast?

A comedy roast is an event where people gather together to talk to and about a special guest, usually someone famous or important. This event usually features "insult comedy," so people might make fun of the guest's character or appearance, but they also say some positive things. The guest knows in advance that this will happen and gives their consent. However, if it goes too far, some of the jokes might be seen as bullying.

Language Point: SO

We use so to add emphasis. It comes before an adjective or an adverb.

  • You're so rich...
  • You're so wealthy that you use $100 bills as napkins.
  • You tell those jokes so well.
  • We use so much with uncountable nouns and so many with plural nouns.

Language Point: SUCH

We use such to add emphasis. It comes before a noun, or an adjective + noun.

  • You're such a fat cat...
  • You're such a big shot you play Monopoly with real cash.
  • It costs such a lot of money that only rich people can afford to eat it.

Cause and Effect

We can use so and such to connect a cause and an effect, often using the word that.

  • Cause: You're so rich.
    Effect: Your wallet counts as carry-on luggage.
    Combined: You're so rich that your wallet counts as carry-on luggage.
  • Cause: You're such a fat cat.
    Effect: Your golf cart has a built-in caviar dispenser.
    Combined: You're such a fat cat that your golf cart has a built-in caviar dispenser.

The Nuances of Humor

The phrases in this lesson rely on exaggeration for comedic effect. The words 'so' and 'such' are used as intensifiers to create a hyperbole. For example, no one is literally 'so old' that their birth certificate is in Roman numerals, but the statement creates a humorous image. This form of insult comedy, as seen in a 'roast', walks a fine line between humor and bullying. What cultural factors might influence whether a joke is perceived as funny or offensive?
