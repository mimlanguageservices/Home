Did you know?

In Ireland, people drive on the left side of the road — the same as the UK. The maximum speed on a motorway is 120 km/h. If you receive 12 penalty points on your licence within 36 months, you are disqualified (banned) from driving.

Exercise 3 – True or False?

Decide if each sentence is true or false based on Irish road rules.

1
The speed limit on a motorway in Ireland is 120 km/h.
TRUE The motorway speed limit is 120 km/h.
2
Learner drivers are allowed on motorways.
FALSE Learner permit holders are not permitted on motorways.
3
In Ireland, you overtake on the right side.
TRUE Since traffic drives on the left, you pass on the right.
4
A green traffic light means you must drive immediately.
FALSE You may proceed only if the way ahead is clear and safe.
5
If you get 12 penalty points in 36 months, you are banned from driving.
TRUE 12 points in 3 years leads to automatic disqualification.

Discussion Questions

Talk with your teacher

1. What is the difference between a learner permit and a driving licence? What restrictions does a learner have?
2. Ireland uses the two-second rule — you must keep at least two seconds of distance from the car in front. Why is this important? Is there a similar rule in your country?
3. The RSA states: "Hit by a car at 60 km/h, 9 out of 10 pedestrians will be killed." What does this tell us about speed? How should this change driver behaviour?
4. What happens in your country if someone drinks alcohol and drives? Is the penalty similar to Ireland's?
5. Imagine you are driving and you come to a yellow box junction but there is traffic ahead. What should you do — and why?
6. Do you think penalty points are an effective way to improve road safety? Can you think of a better system?
